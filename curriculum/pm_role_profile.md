# Product Management Role Profile (Mid-to-Senior Level)

This document outlines the scope, responsibilities, and attributes expected of a mid-to-senior Individual Contributor (IC) in Product Management. This profile is intended to help candidates understand the expectations for this level when preparing for interviews.

---

### 📌 Role Overview
**"As a mid-to-senior Product Manager, you demonstrate mastery of execution, solving ambiguous problems and supporting the strategy."**

You own outcomes that address ambiguous problems in support of product or Business Unit (BU) strategy with accountability for both execution and direction of those projects. You define and launch product solutions for more complex user needs. You synthesize high-level insights from formative research, market gaps, or cutting-edge technical insights, and build a compelling narrative in support of a product decision and direction. You build consensus with cross-functional groups within the product area on overall product direction and influence other teams' projects accordingly. You are a subject-matter specialist for your product area with a deep understanding of users, market, competitive landscape, user insights, and technologies. You also engage in community contributions to improve and sustain the company's culture and operations.

---

### 🎯 Scope and Impact
This section defines the size and type of work owned by a mid-to-senior PM.

#### **1. Contribution**
*   **Personal Impact:** Define a compelling vision for a designated area and organize the cross-functional team to deliver around it, including building processes for a team to enable fast, high-quality execution (e.g., leading PRDs/Design Testing, Dogfoods, Bug burn down activities).
*   **Product/Project Impact:** Deliver outcomes which address difficult problems within the overall product strategy.
*   **Strategy Ownership:** Contribute parts of the product/project strategy, including defining product or feature direction and architecture and driving it to landing (or deprecation).
*   **Resource Management:** Build and manage an actionable product roadmap, contribute to project scoping, managing project priorities, and allocating technical resources within the projects. Make decisions about the team's areas of investment.

#### **2. Challenge**
*   Help solve complex product problems that may have multiple approaches to reach an optimal solution and require foresight to anticipate and address future issues.
*   Direct the resolution of technical, user experience (UX), or privacy-related product design issues.

#### **3. Influence**
*   Develop rich relationships internally and externally to the team, offering guidance and direction to team members.
*   Interact with multiple engineering teams (e.g., back-end, front-end) or complex cross-functional teams (Marketing, UX, Legal, Sales) to translate business needs into product specifications.
*   Perceived by team members (including senior ICs and Tech Leads) as not just a facilitator, but a **full partner/leader in innovation**.
*   Guide the integration of multiple adjacent project timelines, goals, and objectives within the broader product line.

#### **4. Expertise**
*   Utilize comprehensive knowledge of relevant company-wide processes, products, and product management practices (including product inclusion, accessibility, trust and safety, privacy, policy, responsible innovation, efficiency, and reliability) to influence up and across.

### 🛠 General Responsibilities
Examples of potential ways a mid-to-senior PM gets work done:

*   **Create Product Requirements & Co-lead Design:** Independently create Product Requirements Documents (PRDs) that fulfill the roadmap. Work with partner teams to turn PRDs into OKRs (Objectives and Key Results).
*   **Define Product Roadmaps:** Operationalize strategy by independently defining and documenting milestones and company goals using comprehensive knowledge of the competitive landscape.
*   **Develop Product Vision:** Act as a lead contributor to develop and secure buy-in for a product vision that supports the overall narrative.
*   **Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy:** Validate market size and opportunity; identify opportunities in the market landscape.
*   **Lifecycle Management:** Lead launches, maintenance, and retirement. Collaborate with cross-functional partners to ensure approvals by anticipating core dependencies.
*   **Product or Business Metrics:** Define, identify, collect, and track appropriate metrics (quantitative and qualitative) for new or existing products.
*   **Resource Planning & Prioritization:** Initiate, prioritize, and cut projects or features in partnership with engineering teams.

### 🧠 Knowledge and Skills (Expected Proficiency)
The following skills are typically expected to be at the **Proficient** level (capable of applying the skill in typical situations, showing understanding of concepts and processes, with occasional guidance):

*   **Analytical Capability:** Ability to absorb relevant information and develop an opinionated perspective on market/industry and product challenges.
*   **Business Acumen:** Understanding how different strategies impact financial and operational results.
*   **Craft:** Ability to strike a balance between level of detail, prioritization, and execution.
*   **Cross-functional Collaboration:** Interacting effectively across technical and non-technical domains.
*   **Problem Space Understanding:** Synthesizing problem space understanding with technical/business options into credible solutions.
*   **Product Evangelism:** Acting as the voice of the product and narrating a compelling story.
*   **Product Insight:** Creating the long-term purpose and perspective of what the product should be.
*   **Product Lifecycle:** Comprehensive knowledge of the overall process for developing, maintaining, and evolving products.
*   **Strategic Insight & Development:** Establishing or adapting strategies based on customer/competitive intelligence.
*   **User Understanding and Empathy:** Gathering and applying user insights.

### 🤝 Teamwork Attributes
How team members are expected to work together:

*   **Foster Inclusion and Belonging:** Treat everyone with respect, seek different perspectives, and take action against non-inclusive behavior.
*   **Be a Dependable Teammate:** Deliver high-quality work aligned to goals in a timely manner. Adhere to the company's work model.
*   **Collaborate Effectively:** Communicate clearly, listen actively, and proactively offer help to teammates. Adhere to the company's values and Code of Conduct.
