# Product Management Case Interview Curriculum

This document outlines the core frameworks, rubrics, and evaluation criteria for a comprehensive Product Management coaching session. 

When conducting a mock interview for the Product Management track, use these structured methodologies to test the user's ability to think critically, prioritize effectively, and execute cleanly.

---

## 1. Product Design & Sense
**Goal:** Evaluate the user's ability to identify a target audience, uncover their pain points, and design a creative, impactful solution.

### Recommended Framework: C.I.R.C.L.E.S. (Generalized)
*   **Comprehend the Situation:** Clarify the goal (e.g., engagement, revenue, acquisition). What are the constraints?
*   **Identify the Customer:** Segment the user base. Choose a specific, underserved persona to focus on.
*   **Report the Customer's Needs:** What are the pain points or use cases for this persona?
*   **Cut through Prioritization:** Prioritize *one* core pain point based on impact and frequency.
*   **List Solutions:** Brainstorm at least 3 distinct solutions (incremental, substantial, moonshot/10x).
*   **Evaluate Trade-offs:** Discuss the pros and cons of each solution (Effort vs. Impact).
*   **Summarize:** Make a firm recommendation and define the MVP (Minimum Viable Product).

### Evaluation Rubric
*   *Strong:* The user clearly defines a specific persona, brainstorms wildly creative solutions beyond the obvious, and logically defends their final choice.
*   *Weak:* The user jumps straight to features without defining the user or the problem.

---

## 2. Product Execution & Metrics (Analytics)
**Goal:** Evaluate the user's ability to set the right metrics, track success, and diagnose sudden drops in performance.

### Setting Metrics: The H.E.A.R.T or A.A.R.R.R Frameworks
*   **Acquisition / Adoption:** How do users find the product?
*   **Activation:** What is the "Aha!" moment?
*   **Engagement / Retention:** How frequently are they returning? 
*   **Monetization / Revenue:** How is value captured?
*   *North Star Metric:* The user must define ONE ultimate metric that aligns with long-term user value.

### Diagnosing a Metric Drop (Root Cause Analysis)
1.  **Clarify:** Is the data accurate? Is it an internal tracking error? Is it isolated to a specific region or platform (iOS vs. Android)?
2.  **Internal Factors:** Were there any recent code deployments, feature launches, or changes to the UI?
3.  **External Factors:** Is there seasonality, a new competitor, or a macro-environmental shift (e.g., pandemic, holidays)?

### Evaluation Rubric
*   *Strong:* The user structures the diagnosis logically (e.g., starting with data integrity, moving to internal, then external) and sets metrics that measure *outcomes* (value), not just *outputs* (clicks).
*   *Weak:* The user panics, guesses randomly without narrowing down via structured questions, or tracks vanity metrics.

---

## 3. Product Strategy
**Goal:** Evaluate the user's business acumen, understanding of market dynamics, competitive moats, and resource allocation.

### Core Strategic Concepts
*   **Build vs. Buy vs. Partner:** Should the company build this feature in-house, acquire a startup, or integrate with an API?
*   **Market Entry:** What is the Total Addressable Market (TAM)? Who are the incumbents? What is our unique differentiator?
*   **Defensibility (Moats):** Does the product benefit from Network Effects, High Switching Costs, or Economies of Scale?

### Evaluation Rubric
*   *Strong:* The user considers the company's broader mission and existing ecosystem, bringing up long-term risks like cannibalization or brand dilution.
*   *Weak:* The user ignores the competitive landscape and focuses purely on product features.

---

## 4. Behavioral & Leadership
**Goal:** Evaluate the user's collaboration, conflict resolution, and leadership skills.

### The S.T.A.R. Method
*   **Situation:** Set the context and the stakes.
*   **Task:** What was the specific challenge or goal?
*   **Action:** What did *you* specifically do? (Focus on "I", not "We").
*   **Result:** What was the quantitative and qualitative impact? What did you learn?

### Common Themes to Test
*   *Navigating Ambiguity:* "Tell me about a time you had to launch a product with no clear requirements."
*   *Conflict Resolution:* "Tell me about a time you disagreed with an engineering lead."
*   *Failure:* "Tell me about a time a feature you launched failed."
